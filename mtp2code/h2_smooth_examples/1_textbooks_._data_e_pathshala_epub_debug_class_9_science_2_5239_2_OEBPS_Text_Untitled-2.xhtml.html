63: 2.2 What is a Solution

64: A solution is a homogeneous mixture of two or more substances

69: But, we can also have solid solutions (alloys) and gaseous solutions (air)

70: In a solution there is homogeneity at the particle level

71: For example, lemonade tastes the same throughout

74: Alloys: Alloys are mixtures of two or more metals or ametal and a non-metal and cannot be separated into their components by physical methods

75: But still, an alloy is considered as a mixture because it shows the properties of its constituents and can have variable composition

76: For example, brass is a mixture of approximately 30% zinc and 70% copper.

77: A solution has a solvent and a solute as its components

78: The component of the solution that dissolves the other component in it (usually the component present in larger amount) is called the solvent

79: The component of the solution that is dissolved in the solvent (usually present in lesser quantity) is called the solute.

80: Examples:

81: (i) A solution of sugar in water is a solid in liquid solution

84: (iii) Aerated drinks like soda water etc., are gas in liquid solutions

86: (iv) Air is a mixture of gas in gas

87: Air is a homogeneous mixture of a number of gases

88: Its two main constituents are: oxygen (21%) and nitrogen (78%)

89: The other gases are present in very small quantities.
